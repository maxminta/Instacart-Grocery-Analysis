# Instacart-Grocery-Analysis
## Project Overview

This repository contains a comprehensive exploratory data analysis of Instacart’s sales data, focused on uncovering customer purchasing patterns and translating them into actionable marketing and business insights.
With a strong emphasis on real-world decision-making, this project analyzes when customers shop, how much they spend, and what influences their purchasing behavior. The goal is to support data-driven marketing strategies, improve customer targeting, and enhance the effectiveness of promotions and advertising across diverse customer segments.The analysis directly addresses key questions from sales and marketing stakeholders, providing insights that can be used to optimize campaign timing, refine product positioning, and increase customer engagement.

## Key Business Questions
* What are the busiest days of the week and hours of the day for orders?

* When do customers spend the most money?

* How can product price ranges be grouped to better support marketing and promotional strategies?

* Which product categories and departments are most popular?

* How is brand loyalty distributed across the customer base?

* Do ordering behaviors differ by loyalty status?

* Are there regional differences in customer ordering habits?

* Is there a relationship between age and family status in purchasing behavior?

* What meaningful customer segments can be derived from demographic data (age, income, family status)?

* How do ordering patterns vary across different customer profiles?

## Tools & Technologies

The analysis was conducted using Python in Jupyter notebooks, with the following libraries:

Pandas – Data cleaning, manipulation, and analysis

NumPy – Numerical operations and efficient data handling

OS – File and directory management

Matplotlib – Data visualization and custom chart formatting

Seaborn – Statistical and exploratory visualizations

SciPy – Scientific and technical computing
